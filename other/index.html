Monte Carlo Path Tracer

A very basic Monte Carlo path tracer I built to get a better idea of Global Illumination. All surfaces are diffuse, and there are four light bounces. Try it out. Use the mouse to rotate the camera. I used three.js for basic vector math and camera utilities. The path tracer itself is written in GLSL.

The most useful references I used were Inigo Quilez's excellent article: simple pathtracing, Scratchapixel's chapter on Global Illumination, and this similar demo.

Gonville & Caius May Ball

May Balls are formal parties that take place at the end of each academic year in Cambridge. They are known for being some of the most spectacular and extravagant social events in the world.

The Gonville & Caius College May Ball occurs every two years and attracts over a thousand guests. The ball is planned by a May Ball Committee who organize music, food, drink, and entertainment for the guests to enjoy from 7pm through to 5am the following morning.

I have been on the May Ball committee twice – in 2012 and 2014. As a member of the Creative sub-committee, I sourced props and prepared various printed materials. As webmaster, I developed websites and deployed a QR-code–based secure entry system.
