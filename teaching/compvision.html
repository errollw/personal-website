Computer vision

Work for this course includes:

  1. Written exercises – covering a range of course material
  2. Practical exercises – get some hands-on experience with common computer vision algorithms using OpenCV
  3. Past exam questions – discover what is expected in the exam

Credit for the practical exercises goes to Christian Richardt, Tadas Baltrušaitis (also creator of this supervision plan), and Lech Świrski.

Submission of practical exercises

The practical work consists of "filling in the blanks" to complete Python scripts.

For each week you will have a separate Python script. Please name these in the form <crsid>_<exerciseN>.py. E.g. my practical work for the first week would be eww23_exercise1.py

Index

    Supervision work

    Please complete the following written exercises, practical exercises, and past paper questions:

    Supervision 1

    This supervision concerns some of the biological principles behind vision, and introduces convolution – a fundamental computer vision operation.

    • Practical exercise 1 – convolution
    • Written exercises 1 to 5
    • 2009 Paper 8 Question 4 part (c)
    • 2010 Paper 9 Question 4 part (b)

    Supervision 2

    This supervision covers how image structure can be extracted using filtering, the important Laplacian of a Gaussian filter, and other techniques for extracting image features.

    • Practical exercise 2 – edge detection
    • Written exercises 6 to 9
    • 2010 Paper 8 Question 4 part (b)

    Supervision 3

    This supervision introduces how stereo, motion, and color descriptors can be generated and used, e.g. to create a panorama.

    • Practical exercise 3 – panorama stitching
    • Written exercises 11 and 12
    • 2010 Paper 9 Question 4 part (a)
    • 2010 Paper 8 Question 4 part (a)

    Supervision 4

    The final supervision focusses on classification problems – OCR and face recognition, and includes some exam paper practice.

    • Practical exercise 4 – Naïve Bayes for machine learning
    • Written exercises 15 and 17
    • 2011 Paper 8 Question 5

    Please also prepare any last questions you have about the course.
