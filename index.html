About me

I am a PhD student in the Rainbow Group (graphics & interaction) at the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory, supervised by Professor Peter Robinson.

My research interests include pervasive gaze tracking, attentive user interfaces, large displays, and ubiquitous computing.

My preferred method of contact is email: eww23@cam.ac.uk.

Brief CV

I graduated from Cambridge University in July 2013 with both a MEng (Distinction) in the Computer Science Tripos (CST) Part III and a BA (First Class) in CST Part II.

For my MEng project I developed a gaze tracking system suited for commodity portable devices, taking hardware limitations and typical use environments into account (supervisor: Dr Andreas Bulling).

For my BA project I devloped data-structures and algorithms for procedurally generating volumetric fractals in a sparse-voxel-octree, and rendering it in real-time independent of the scale it is explored at. (supervisor: Dr Alex Benton).

I have recently interned at the Cambridge Computer Lab, Qualcomm Research Cambridge, and Credit Suisse.
