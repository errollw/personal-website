About

I am a PhD student in the Rainbow Group (graphics & interaction) at the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory.

My supervisor is Professor Peter Robinson.

Research interests include gaze tracking, attentive user interfaces, large displays, and networked public-display communication.

    Brief CV

    I am currently a PhD student in the Rainbow Group (graphics & interaction) at the University of Cambridge Computer Laboratory. I graduated from Cambridge University in July 2013 with both a MEng (Distinction) in CST Part III and a BA (First Class) in CST Part II.

    For my MEng project I developed a gaze tracking system suited for commodity portable devices, taking hardware limitations and typical use environments into account (supervisor: Dr Andreas Bulling).

    For my BA project I devloped data-structures and algorithms for procedurally generating volumetric fractals in a sparse-voxel-octree, and rendering it in real-time independent of the scale it is explored at. (supervisor: Dr Alex Benton).
